## II. Lancaster Family Development Line (Centered on the Growth and Dilemmas of Family Members)

### Starting Point and Character Foundation
The family is centered on courage, loyalty, and family reputation. Intergenerational inheritance emphasizes the family rule of "protecting the land and passing down the coat of arms."
Elders emphasize etiquette and honor, while the younger generation seeks self-identity within this tradition.

### Intergenerational Conflict and Emotional Entanglements
Two main characters (fictionalized young members) waver between marriage and loyalty. Trust and betrayal among family members become the emotional driving force of the story.
Competition within the family is not entirely a cold, hard power struggle; it manifests more as anxiety about the future, a desire for family security, and the pursuit of personal freedom.

### Key Turning Points (Emotional and Fateful Inflection Points)
A young member takes a wrong path due to love or a misunderstanding of "family interests," leading to strained family relationships. Another member chooses to sacrifice themselves to protect the family's reputation, leaving behind long-term trauma and opportunities for reconciliation.

### Ending Vision
The family's rifts are not fully repaired, but through shared memories and rituals of reconciliation, new common ground and methods of inheritance are found, planting seeds of hope for future generations.

## III. York Family Development Line (Driven by Calmness, Strategy, and Emotional Choices)

### Starting Point and Character Foundation
The York family emphasizes independence, wisdom, and the pursuit of fairness. Family members are known for their rationality, meticulous planning abilities, and skill in handling complex interpersonal relationships.

### Intergenerational Inheritance and Emotional Choices
The younger generation seeks a balance between family duty and personal ideals; marriage and alliances become emotional trials for personal growth.
Some within the family choose to overcome hardness with softness, using relational networks to resolve conflicts; others, under pressure, adhere to personal beliefs at a cost.

### Key Turning Points (Emotional and Fateful Inflection Points)
A complex marriage arrangement leads to misunderstandings and conflicts, prompting family members to re-examine the meaning of "loyalty."
Through friendship, trust, and forgiveness, opportunities for reunification are found, opening new paths for shared life.

### Ending Vision
After enduring hardships, the York family learns to maintain family integrity in a more inclusive way while establishing new models of inheritance for the future.
