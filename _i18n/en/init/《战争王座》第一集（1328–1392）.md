## **《The Fight for the Crown》（circa 1328–1392）Summary**

The first episode focuses on **the beginning of the Hundred Years' War**, detailing how a dispute over royal succession between England and France escalated into a century-long bloody conflict. Key figures in this episode include:

- **King Edward III of England**
- **King Philip VI of France**
- **The Black Prince (Edward, the Black Prince)**
- **King John II of France**

The following is the main narrative outline of this episode:

## 👑 I. The Succession Crisis: The Spark of the Hundred Years' War

- After the direct male line of the French Capetian dynasty ended, a dispute over the throne arose.
- **King Edward III** of England claimed the French throne through his maternal lineage.
- The French nobility refused to recognize the English king and instead elevated **Philip VI** to the throne.
- Tensions between the two kingdoms rapidly escalated, bringing them to the brink of war.

## ⚔️ II. England's Military Advantage: The Longbow and Mobile Warfare

- Edward III led his army to land in France, initiating the Hundred Years' War.
- The English army achieved overwhelming victories in several battles thanks to their **longbowmen** and flexible tactics.
- The most famous battles include:
  - **The Battle of Crécy (1346)**
  - **The Battle of Poitiers (1356)**
- The traditional heavy cavalry charges of French knights repeatedly failed against English tactics.

## 🛡️ III. The Rise of the Black Prince

- Edward III's son, **the Black Prince**, became England's most brilliant military commander.
- He captured the French king, **John II**, at the Battle of Poitiers, shocking all of Europe.
- England reached the peak of its power, while France plunged into a deep crisis.

## 💰 IV. France's Decline and Social Turmoil

- The capture of the king led to France's financial collapse and intensified conflicts between the nobility and the common people.
- Peasant revolts (such as the **Jacquerie**) erupted, bringing social order to the brink of collapse.
- Factional struggles within the French royal family laid the groundwork for subsequent chaos.

## 🌧️ V. The Dual Blows of Plague and War

- **The Black Death** swept across Europe, causing massive population losses in both France and England.
- The war temporarily slowed, but hostility between the two nations persisted.
- France attempted to restore royal authority, but internal conflicts and external pressures remained immense.

# 🎯 **One-Sentence Summary**

The first episode tells the story of: **How the Hundred Years' War erupted due to a succession dispute, how England achieved early victories through military superiority, and how France struggled amidst defeat, plague, and internal turmoil, setting the stage for the subsequent era of the "Mad King" and the rise of Joan of Arc.**

The Black Prince, Edward, was renowned for his courage and composure. At the Battle of Crécy, he employed mobile tactics to suppress the French forces, securing a crucial victory and winning the loyalty of his troops. In 1356 at Poitiers, he skillfully used terrain and ambushes to unexpectedly rout his opponents and capture King John II of France, shocking Europe. This victory temporarily elevated England to the peak of its power, but it also brought the heavy burden of consolidating royal authority and the shadow of post-war trauma. Thus, the Black Prince became the embodiment of military and political authority—a symbol of glory as well as a bearer of the shadows of war—laying a solid foundation for the subsequent Anglo-French rivalry.
