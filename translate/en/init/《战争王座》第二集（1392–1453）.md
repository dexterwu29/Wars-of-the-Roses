# 📘 "Throne of War" Episode 2 (1392–1453)

## **"The Mad King" Content Summary (Plot Recap Version)**

This episode focuses on the **mid-Hundred Years' War** power struggle between France and England, while depicting the fates of two key figures:

- **King Charles VI of France ("The Mad King")**
- **Joan of Arc (Johanna von Orleans)**

Here is the core narrative thread of this episode:

## 🔥 I. The Hundred Years' War Continues to Tear Europe Apart

- The Hundred Years' War between France and England intensified during this period.
- The war caused **hundreds of thousands of deaths**, deepening the hatred between the two royal houses.
- England held military superiority, while the French monarchy gradually weakened.

## 👑 II. The Tragedy of King Charles VI of France: From Love to Madness

- Charles VI was deeply loved in his youth and was called "Charles the Beloved."
- He married **Isabeau de Bavière**, a princess from Bavaria.
- However, soon after, Charles VI suffered a mental breakdown, exhibiting violent and delusional symptoms, even failing to recognize his own family.
- The king's madness plunged French politics into chaos, with noble factions vying for power.

## ⚔️ III. Royal Infighting and English Expansion

- Charles VI's madness split the French royal family into two major factions:
  - **The Armagnacs**
  - **The Bourguignons**
- England seized the opportunity to invade France, achieving several major victories.
- The French Dauphin (the future Charles VII) was forced to flee, and the throne was in jeopardy.

## ✨ IV. The Revelation of a Peasant Girl: The Arrival of Joan of Arc

- At France's darkest hour, a peasant girl named **Joan of Arc** claimed to have received divine inspiration.
- She successfully persuaded the Dauphin to let her lead an army to lift the siege of Orléans.
- Joan's appearance greatly boosted French morale, becoming a turning point in the war.
- She helped the Dauphin be crowned **Charles VII** at Reims, restoring the French monarchy.

## 🔥 V. A Tragic End: Immortality at the Stake

- Joan was eventually captured by England and its allies.
- She was accused of heresy and witchcraft and put on trial.
- In 1431, Joan was burned at the stake in Rouen.
- Although her body was destroyed, she became France's eternal national heroine.

This episode tells the story: **Amid the bloodshed of the Hundred Years' War, the madness of King Charles VI of France plunged the nation into chaos, while the appearance of Joan of Arc reignited France's hope, ultimately propelling the Dauphin to the throne.**
